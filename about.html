Why I love running

       My attempt of drawing myself running      

I initially started to run on a regular basis to get fit. Then I enjoyed other aspects along the way. Over the years, running has taught me:

  • Mental strength and to be comfortable with being uncomfortable by continuing through the pain, by anticipating the joy felt at the finish.

  • To stop comparing myself to others and to be proud of my achievements, small or large. I finish marathons amid the slowest group and I used to feel down when looking at my finish times. As fellow runners repeatedly dissuaded me to compare myself to others and told me to simply be proud of my accomplishments, I learnt to enjoy every aspect of running.

  • To be grateful for the volunteers, musicians and cheerers along the way. Volunteers for handing out drinks with the occasional cheering (including non-official volunteers), musicians for playing and giving us extra boost, and cheerers for their enthusiasm. I have no idea what drives them to stand for hours in the cold or rain to encourage random strangers. All the same, they give me the courage to cheer strangers in return, as I know how uplifting it is when you're hurting to see someone hail your name with a big smile.
